CandBase- A comprehensive candida genome database

Welcome to candida database !!

Candida is a genus of fungi and is mostly responsible for fungal infections all over the world. Candidiasis, a fungal infection, is caused by candida species. Many candida species have been discovered till now of which candida albicans are the most common species and responsible for most fungal infections. Genome of most of the candida species has been sequenced. Candidiasis is predominantly common in immuno-compromised individuals. Candida species are found as normal microflora in healthy people. The immune system and beneficial bacteria (probiotics) control these species. Candidiasis occurs when number of candida increases due to lowering of probiotics leading to decrease in immune system.

Candida species are a ubiquitous yeast fungi. These are found in three diverse classifications which makes it an important and diverse branch of fungi to be studied. This database covers over 15 candida species and contains an overview about each of these 15 species alongwith their classification into pathogenic, opportunistic and non-pathogenic species. The genes related for their pathogenicity are also included alongwith their genome and nucleotides-rRNA and mRNA. The purpose of this database is to provide a platform wherein maximum candida species have been covered with their required genome and nucleotide information and their classification. Few tools like BLAST,Clustal X2 have also been included for analysis.
